# FINDINGS — Student Performance Analysis

## Dataset Overview

The dataset includes approximately 2000 student records with key variables such as GPA, study hours, attendance, and scholarship status.

Some missing values were handled as follows:
- commute_minutes (~10%) filled with median
- study_hours_weekly (~5%) removed
- scholarship (~20%) filled with "None"

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Study Hours vs GPA

Students who study more hours tend to achieve higher GPAs. There is a clear positive relationship between study time and academic performance.

---

### 2. Attendance Impact

Attendance plays a strong role in GPA. Students with higher attendance percentages generally perform better academically.

---

### 3. Scholarship Distribution

Students with scholarships tend to have slightly higher GPAs on average, indicating a possible relationship between performance and scholarship eligibility.

---

### 4. Combined Effect

The best-performing students are those who combine:
- High study hours
- High attendance

This suggests that consistency and effort both contribute significantly to success.

---

## Conclusion

Overall, the analysis shows that both study habits and attendance are strong predictors of academic performance. These insights can help educators identify areas to support students and improve outcomes.
